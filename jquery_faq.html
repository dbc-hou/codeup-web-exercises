National Parks That I Have Visited

Big Bend (Texas)

4 Fast Facts:

  • It has mountains you can drive up (carefully!).
  • The closest municipality of any size is Alpine, which is two hours' drive away!
  • A good side trip is the Marfa Lights viewing platform between Marfa and Alpine.
  • A college friend of mine died there.

Yellowstone (Wyoming, Idaho, and Montana)

4 Fast Facts:

  • It is the first US National Park, established in 1872.
  • It is larger than Delaware and Rhode Island combined.
  • The largest geyser in the park, Old Faithful, erupts every 90 minutes on average.
  • The bears and wolves will leave you alone, unless you leave food out unguarded.

Acadia (Maine)

4 Fast Facts:

  • It is the only national park in New England.
  • The town of Bar Harbor is on its northeastern edge (great for seafood lovers).
  • Most of it is on Mount Desert Island.
  • On our visit, 4th of July weekend of 2001, the alternator in my van gave out.
